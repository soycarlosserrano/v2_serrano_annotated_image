Learning to Walk

The challenges of living with prostheses

By Carlos Serrano

Late on the night of Aug. 6, 2015, Kevin Lu was riding his motorcycle home. It was his birthday. About 11:30 p.m, on the Whitestone Expressway in Queens, N.Y., an 18-wheeler struck him and ran over him. He does not remember anything about the accident, but when he woke up in the hospital, doctors already had amputated his legs. Now 32, Kevin Lu has been using prosthetic legs for nine months and is working hard to learn to walk again. He goes to the Rusk Rehabilitation Center of NYU Langone Medical Center twice a week for therapy. He also trains at Prosthetics in Motion in midtown Manhattan, where he got his prostheses.
